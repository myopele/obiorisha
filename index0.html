Obi Orisha Introduction

orientation & intent

This mini-series is designed to help students transition from “basic 4-cowrie answers” toward a more grounded Obi practice that includes:

  • Clearer framing (what is being asked, and what is not).
  • Consistent orientation and casting discipline.
  • Ethics: establishing Ire and identifying Ibi without fear-mongering.
  • Validation: confirming meaning through structured methods, not guesswork.

The goal is not to rush students into “advanced priesthood claims,” but to establish a repeatable, teachable foundation.

  • Daily practice builds pattern recognition.
  • Logs reveal bias and strengthen accuracy.
  • Ethical framing reduces harm and confusion.

Weekly Plan

a suggested structure (adaptable)
Week Focus Practice & Deliverable
Week 1 Orientation & Question Framing 16 casts/day. Practice writing questions with clarity (who/what/when/why). Deliverable: 1-page log summary.
Week 2 Establishing Ire (no Ibi) 16 casts/day. Record outcomes and check your emotional bias. Deliverable: examples of Ire interpretation + ethical note.
Week 3 Establishing Ibi 16 casts/day. Learn how to identify Ibi without panic; focus on remedy thinking. Deliverable: Ibi logs + proposed corrective actions.
Week 4 Validation of the Process Compare your reading to outcomes, verify meaning via structure. Deliverable: validation worksheet + 3 examples.
Week 5 Obi Abata / Obi Orisha Families Begin tracking families and attachments. Deliverable: family chart notes + photos.
Week 6 Integration & Ethics Create a personal ethical standard for reading others. Deliverable: 1-page ethics & consent statement.

Tip: Keep your logs consistent — date, question, cast, interpretation, outcome, and reflections.

Certification Readiness

what SGI looks for

Minimum Requirements

  • Consistent daily casting and logging.
  • Demonstrated Ire/Ibi differentiation.
  • Ability to explain reasoning (not just “guess”).
  • Ethical boundaries for reading others.

Submission Package

  • Photos of select casts.
  • Weekly log summaries.
  • Interpretation notes showing improvement.
  • Short statement: your ethical code + limitations.
